Dolphins

Dolphins deliberately handle pufferfish causing them to release toxins as a defense mechanism. These toxins can be deadly in high doses, but also have a narcotic effect – and are a powerful hallucinogenic, which dolphins appear to enjoy.

  • Scientific name: Stenella longirostris
  • Average length: 4.2 – 7.7 ft.
  • Average lifespan: 20 years

  • Habitat: Warm ocean water

Dolphins are small-toothed cetaceans easily recognizable by their curved mouths, which give them a permanent “smile.” There are 36 dolphin species, found in every ocean. Dolphins have smooth, rubbery skin and are usually colored in some mixture of black, white, and gray.

Elephants

Elephants will return to the place where family members died and stand in silence over the bones, sometimes bowing their heads.

  • Scientific name: Loxodonta
  • Average length: 18 - 21 ft.
  • Average lifespan: 70 years
  • Habitat: African savannas

The largest living land animal, elephants are characterized by its long trunk (elongated upper lip and nose), columnar legs, and huge head with temporal glands and wide, flat ears. Since they have few enemies to fear, elephants are usually peaceful and easygoing. They show great affection for one another, and females spend their lives as members of a family herd.

Giraffes

Giraffes only need 5 to 30 minutes of sleep in a 24-hour period! They often achieve that in quick naps that may last only a minute or two at a time

  • Scientific name: Giraffa
  • Average length: 9.75 - 15 ft
  • Average lifespan: 26 years
  • Habitat: African grasslands

The tallest of all living land animals, giraffes are easily identified by their long necks. Most giraffes have tan, white or yellow coats that are spotted with brown, square shapes which are similar to fingerprints as no two coats are the same. Their main source of food is acacia, a tall tree which thrives in African savannas.
